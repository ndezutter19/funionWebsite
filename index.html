Game Review: Rock Paper Scissors By: Angus J. ↓

In the 87 hours that it took me to beat Rock Paper Scissors, I was put through an absolute wringer of emotion: Anger as I was beaten down by its toughest challenges, exhilaration when I finally overcame them, and a fair amount of sorrow for the mountains of times I lost against my friends. But more than anything else I was in near-constant awe – from the many absolutely jaw-dropping strategies, the sheer scope of an absolutely enormous number of possibilities, 9, the frequently harrowing enemies, and the way in which Rock Paper Scissors nearly always rewarded my ingenuity with either an interesting challenge, a valuable experience, or something even greater. The ancient Chinese take the ball that tic-tac-toe got rolling and run with it, creating an interesting and big-brained strategy game, while also being extremely simple. It shouldn’t be a surprise to anyone that Rock Paper Scissors ended up as one of the most unforgettable gaming experiences I’ve ever had.
